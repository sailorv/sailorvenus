The waning gibbous moon is sometimes called the Moon of Retribution, a name which provokes a sense of vengeance and harm. Under the light of this moon, it is believed that the consequences of one's past actions will be revealed.

And yet, on the other side of reckoning is healing. In the tarot, the waning gibbous moon is represented by The Star, the card that immediately follows The Tower. It depicts a woman pouring two ewers of water into a stream under a starlit sky. She is meant to give a sense of hope after The Tower - and she is an appropriate avatar for the phase of healing that follows the lunacy of the full moon.

Whether I mean to or not, I find myself reflecting a lot during this phase - so much so that I think I generally journal more under this phase than any other.
